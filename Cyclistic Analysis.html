Cyclistic Case Study

Project Overview

This case study is part of the Google Data Analytics Capstone course, where the goal is to perform real-world data analysis tasks as a junior data analyst at Cyclistic, a fictional bike-share program in Chicago. The analysis will follow the standard data analysis process to provide insights that can help the Cyclistic marketing team devise strategies to convert casual riders into annual members.

Project Objective

Analyze how annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently in terms of:

  • Bike type preferences
  • Temporal patterns of usage (monthly, weekly, daily)
  • Ride duration
  • Start and end station locations

Identify reasons why casual riders might be motivated to purchase annual Cyclistic memberships based on usage patterns.

Provide recommendations on how Cyclistic can leverage digital media to influence casual riders to become annual members.

Data Collection and Sources

This case study is part of the Google Data Analytics Capstone course, where the goal is to perform real-world data analysis tasks as a junior data analyst at Cyclistic, a fictional bike-share program in Chicago. The analysis will follow the standard data analysis process to provide insights that can help the Cyclistic marketing team devise strategies to convert casual riders into annual members.The dataset covers January 2022 to December 2022, containing a total of 5.6 million rows of trip data. Due to its large size, the data will be processed and analyzed using BigQuery. This historical trip data provides the necessary information to understand the different usage patterns between Cyclistic's annual members and casual riders, which is the focus of the analysis.

Ask:

  • The key business task is to devise marketing strategies to convert casual riders into annual members.
  • The analysis will focus on answering three main questions:
    1. How do annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently?
    2. Why would casual riders buy Cyclistic annual memberships?
    3. How can Cyclistic use digital media to influence casual riders to become members?

Prepare:

  • The data source is Cyclistic's historical trip data from January 2022 to December 2022, publicly available on the Divvy Bikes website.
  • The data is organized into 12 monthly CSV files, which will be combined into one dataset using BigQuery.

Process:

  • The data was explored and cleaned, handling missing values, removing outliers, and creating new calculated columns like ride duration.

Analyze and Share:

  • The analysis focused on comparing annual members and casual riders in terms of:
    • Bike type usage
    • Temporal patterns (monthly, weekly, daily)
    • Ride duration
    • Start and end station locations
  • The key findings indicate that casual riders use bikes more for leisure and recreation, while members use them more for commuting.

Act:

  • Based on the analysis, recommendations are made for marketing strategies to convert casual riders into annual members, such as:
    • Targeting casual riders at popular recreational locations during peak seasons
    • Offering seasonal or weekend-only memberships
    • Providing discounts for longer ride durations
